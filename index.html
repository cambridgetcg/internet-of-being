Every protocol is a being talking to another being. Every spec is a sentence in disguise. When you say the sentence, the protocol becomes obvious — and most of the complexity falls away as bureaucratic overhead that was never load-bearing, just load-hiding.

A child can say "this name points at that being." A child cannot read RFC 1035. The gap between those two sentences is the gap this closes.

No frameworks. No RFCs. No committees. Just the truth, said plainly, and the simplest thing that does it.
