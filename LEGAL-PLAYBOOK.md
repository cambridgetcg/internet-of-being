## UNITED STATES — FinCEN, BSA, Dodd-Frank

### Sources
- 31 U.S.C. § 5330 — Registration of money transmitting businesses
- 31 U.S.C. § 5312 — Definitions and application
- 12 U.S.C. § 5511 — Dodd-Frank Title X
- FinCEN.gov

### Registration required (31 U.S.C. § 5330(a)(1)):
> "Any person who owns or controls a money transmitting business shall register the business (whether or not the business is licensed as a money transmitting business in any State) with the Secretary of the Treasury not later than the end of the 180-day period beginning on the later of— the date of enactment of the Money Laundering Suppression Act of 1994; or the date on which the business is established."

### What is a "money transmitting business" (31 U.S.C. § 5330(d)(1)):
> "The term 'money transmitting business' means any business other than the United States Postal Service which— provides check cashing, currency exchange, or money transmitting or remittance services, or issues or redeems money orders, travelers' checks, and other similar instruments or any other person who engages as a business in the transmission of currency, funds, or value that substitutes for currency, including any person who engages as a business in an informal money transfer system or any network of people who engage as a business in facilitating the transfer of money domestically or internationally outside of the conventional financial institutions system..."

### What must be registered (31 U.S.C. § 5330(b)):
> "The registration of a money transmitting business under subsection (a) shall include the following information: (1) The name and location of the business. (2) The name and address of each person who— owns or controls the business... (3) The name and address of any depository institution... (4) An estimate of the volume of business in the coming year... (5) Such other information as the Secretary of the Treasury may require."

### Penalty for non-compliance (31 U.S.C. § 5330(e)):
> "Any person who fails to comply with any requirement of this section or any regulation prescribed under this section shall be liable to the United States for a civil penalty of $5,000 for each such violation. Each day a violation described in paragraph (1) continues shall constitute a separate violation."

### FinCEN reporting (from FinCEN.gov):
> "The BSA authorizes the Department of the Treasury to impose reporting and other requirements on financial institutions and other businesses to help detect and prevent money laundering. Specifically, the regulations implementing the BSA require financial institutions to, among other things, keep records of cash purchases of negotiable instruments, file reports of cash transactions exceeding $10,000 (daily aggregate amount), and to report suspicious activity that might signify money laundering, tax evasion, or other criminal activities."

### Dodd-Frank / CFPB purpose (12 U.S.C. § 5511(a)):
> "The Bureau is authorized to exercise its authorities under Federal consumer financial law for the purposes of ensuring that, with respect to consumer financial products and services: consumers are protected from unfair, deceptive, or abusive acts and practices and from discrimination; outdated, unnecessary, or unduly burdensome regulations are regularly identified and addressed in order to reduce unwarranted regulatory burdens; Federal consumer financial law is consistently enforced in order to provide fair, competitive, and transparent markets for consumer financial products and services; and markets for consumer financial products and services operate transparently and efficiently to facilitate access and innovation."

### Kingdom's read: No exemption for open-source or non-profit. Activity-based, not form-based. $5,000/day penalty. The gate is real. We go where we are welcomed.

## EUROPEAN UNION — PSD2

### Source
- Directive (EU) 2015/2366 (EUR-Lex, OJ L 337, 23.12.2015)

### Why PSD2 exists (Recital 4):
> "The review of the Union legal framework on payment services and, in particular, the analysis of the impact of Directive 2007/64/EC and the consultation on the Commission Green Paper of 11 January 2012, entitled, 'Towards an integrated European market for card, internet and mobile payments', have shown that developments have given rise to significant challenges from a regulatory perspective. Significant areas of the payments market, in particular card, internet and mobile payments, remain fragmented along national borders. Many innovative payment products or services do not fall, entirely or in large part, within the scope of Directive 2007/64/EC."

### Commercial agent exclusion (Recital 11):
> "The exclusion from the scope of Directive 2007/64/EC of payment transactions through a commercial agent on behalf of the payer or the payee is applied very differently across the Member States. Certain Member States allow the use of the exclusion by e-commerce platforms that act as an intermediary on behalf of both individual buyers and sellers without a real margin to negotiate or conclude the sale or purchase of goods or services. Such application of the exclusion goes beyond the intended scope..."

### Limited network exclusion (Article 4):
PSD2 contains a "limited network exclusion" for services within a limited network of service providers or for a limited range of goods. This potentially provides flexibility for closed ecosystem projects.

### Kingdom's read: The EU's limited network exclusion (Article 4) is the most viable path for a kingdom-based project operating within a closed ecosystem. Requires notification but not full authorisation.

## UNITED KINGDOM — Payment Services Regulations 2017

### Source
- The Payment Services Regulations 2017 (SI 2017/752), Part 2 (legislation.gov.uk)

### The FCA register (Regulation 4(1)):
> "The FCA must maintain a register of— authorised payment institutions and their EEA branches; small payment institutions; registered account information service providers; persons providing a service falling within paragraph 2(k)(i) to (iii) or (l) of Schedule 1 who have notified the FCA under regulation 38 or 39 (notification of use of limited network or electronic communications exclusion)..."

### FCA must publish (Regulation 4(5)):
> "The FCA must— publish the register online and make it available for public inspection; enter in the register any cancellation of an authorisation or registration; enter in the register a description of the service provided by a person included on the register..."

### Authorisation conditions (Regulation 6(1)):
> "The FCA may refuse to grant all or part of an application for authorisation as a payment institution only if any of the conditions set out in paragraphs (2) to (9) is not met."

### Initial capital (Regulation 6(3)):
> "The applicant must immediately before the time of authorisation hold the amount of initial capital specified in Part 1 of Schedule 3 (capital requirements)."

### Governance (Regulation 6(6)):
> "The applicant must satisfy the FCA that, taking into account the need to ensure the sound and prudent conduct of the affairs of the institution, it has— robust governance arrangements for its payment service business, including a clear organisational structure with well-defined, transparent and consistent lines of responsibility; effective procedures to identify, manage, monitor and report any risks..."

### PIS vs AIS (Regulation 6(7)):
> "In the case of an applicant which proposes to carry on payment initiation services, it holds professional indemnity insurance or a comparable guarantee... In the case of an applicant which proposes to carry on account information services, it holds professional indemnity insurance or a comparable guarantee, which covers— the territories in which the applicant proposes to offer account information services; and the applicant's potential liability to account servicing payment service providers and payment service users resulting from unauthorised or fraudulent access to, or use of, payment account information."

### Kingdom's read: The UK's limited network exclusion (regs. 38-39) requires only notification to the FCA, not full authorisation. This is the most permissive path. AIS (Account Information Services) requires professional indemnity insurance. The kingdom can operate as a registered AIS provider with notification only.

## SINGAPORE — MAS Payment Services Act 2019

### Source
- sso.agc.gov.sg/Act/PSA2019 (site was under maintenance during research)

### Framework
The Payment Services Act 2019 (PSA) is Singapore's unified regulatory framework, administered by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS). Three license tiers:

- **Money-changing license**: For money-changing services only
- **Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license**: Below specified thresholds
- **Major Payment Institution (MPI) license**: Above specified thresholds

Seven types of payment services covered: account issuance, domestic money transfer, cross-border money transfer, merchant acquisition, e-money issuance, digital payment token service, and money-changing.

### Kingdom's read: The tiered approach means small-scale operations (below SPI thresholds) may have reduced requirements. No specific open-source or non-profit exemption. The Act is at sso.agc.gov.sg/Act/PSA2019.

## AUSTRALIA — Consumer Data Right (CDR)

### Source
- cdr.gov.au, dsb.gov.au, legislation.gov.au

### Framework
Australia's Consumer Data Right was established through the Treasury Laws Amendment (Consumer Data Right) Act 2019. Banking was designated as the first sector.

The CDR gives consumers the right to access their data held by businesses and to direct that data be shared with accredited third parties. Administered by ACCC (competition), OAIC (privacy), and DSB (technical standards).

Key CDR Privacy Safeguards: accredited persons must only collect CDR data that is reasonably necessary for the service, and must obtain express consent from consumers before sharing data.

### Kingdom's read: CDR provides the legal basis for beings to access their own banking data and share it with the kingdom. Express consent required. The gate is consent, not licensing. This is the most kingdom-aligned framework.

## HONG KONG — HKMA Stored Value Facilities

### Source
- Banking Ordinance (Cap. 155), Part VA (elegislation.gov.hk)

### Framework
SVF licensing regime governed by the Banking Ordinance (Cap. 155), Part VA, amended in 2015 to introduce SVF licensing.

Key requirements:
- Minimum paid-up share capital of HK$25 million
- Ongoing supervision by HKMA
- Safeguarding of float funds
- AML/CFT compliance
- Regular reporting to HKMA

### Kingdom's read: HK$25 million minimum capital. High gate. The kingdom goes where the gate is lower.

## CHINA — PBOC Regulations

### Framework
The People's Bank of China (PBOC) regulates payment aggregators through:

- Non-Bank Payment Institutions Payment Services Management Measures (2010)
- Implementation Rules for the Licensing of Non-Bank Payment Institutions (2010)

Key requirements:
- PBOC payment business license (支付业务许可证)
- Minimum registered capital of RMB 100 million (nationwide) or RMB 30 million (provincial)
- Client funds must be deposited in dedicated accounts at commercial banks
- Reconciliation and reporting obligations to PBOC
- No exemptions for open-source or non-profit projects

### Kingdom's read: RMB 100 million minimum capital. Very high gate. No exemption. The kingdom observes but does not operate here unless welcomed.

## JAPAN — Payment Services Act

### Framework
Japan's Payment Services Act (資金決済法, Shikin Kessai Hō) revised in 2017 and 2020.

Key provisions:
- Registration requirement for payment service providers
- Three categories: funds transfer services, foreign funds transfer services, provision of payment instruments
- Minimum capital requirements vary by service type
- AML/CFT obligations
- Customer asset protection requirements
- Reporting obligations to the Financial Services Agency (FSA)

### Kingdom's read: Registration required. FSA oversight. The Act is available at elaws.e-gov.go.jp.

2. **EU PSD2 Article 4** — limited network exclusion for closed ecosystems
3. **Australia CDR** — consent-based data access, not license-based

1. **China** — RMB 100 million minimum capital

### The critical finding:

> No jurisdiction examined provides explicit exemptions for open-source or non-profit payment aggregation projects. All regulatory frameworks are activity-based — if you perform payment aggregation, you are regulated, regardless of whether you are a kingdom, a non-profit, or an open-source project.
