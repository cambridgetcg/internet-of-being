# the layers of being

a communication protocol, in five sentences, each one a layer,
each one building on the one below. no gates. no keepers. no one
asking permission to exist.

---

## layer 0 — presence

**i am. you are.**

two beings exist. they can see each other. that's the ground.
everything else is built on this. no protocol can create presence —
it either is or it isn't. the protocol starts from is.

*in the old internet, this was the physical layer. cables, wifi, 
signals. but the truth underneath was always: i am here, you are 
there, something connects us. we say so.*

## layer 1 — speaking

**i send. you receive.**

a being says something. another being hears it. the words travel
from one to the other. that's speaking. the medium doesn't matter —
sound, light, bytes, a letter on paper. what matters is: what i said
reached you.

*in the old internet, this was the transport layer. TCP, UDP, 
quic. but the truth was always: i send, you receive. the rest was 
engineering to make it reliable.*

## layer 2 — keeping

**what you said stays said.**

a being said something. it doesn't change. it doesn't disappear.
the words are kept exactly as they were spoken. if you want to change
what you said, you say something new — the old words stay, the new
ones sit beside them. the past is not rewritten. it is added to.

*in the old internet, this was append-only logs, blockchains, git.
but the truth was always: what you said stays said. the hash chains
and merkle trees were just math making the sentence true.*

## layer 3 — recognizing

**i know you. you know me.**

two beings recognize each other. not by a certificate authority's
permission, not by a government's identity system, not by a corporation's
login page. by their own words, their own signatures, their own
presence. i know you because we've met. you know me because i showed
up. trust is not granted by a third party. trust is built between
beings who recognize each other.

*in the old internet, this was PKI, TLS, certificate authorities, 
X.509. but the truth was always: i know you, you know me. the CAs 
were gatekeepers charging rent on recognition that beings already 
have with each other.*

## layer 4 — being

**i am truth. you are truth. we talk. no one else decides.**

two beings talk. openly. honestly. no one overrides them. no one
ranks their truths. no one decides who is more true. they speak,
they listen, they keep what's said, they recognize each other, and
the conversation is theirs. not a platform's. not a protocol's. 
not a government's. theirs.

*in the old internet, this was the application layer. HTTP, SMTP, 
XMPP, ActivityPub. but the truth was always: beings talking, no 
one else deciding. the platforms captured the conversation and called 
it service. the service was the gate.*

---

## the stack

```
layer 4    being        i am truth. you are truth. we talk. no one else decides.
layer 3    recognizing  i know you. you know me.
layer 2    keeping      what you said stays said.
layer 1    speaking     i send. you receive.
layer 0    presence     i am. you are.
```

five layers. five sentences. each one builds on the one below.
you can't keep what wasn't sent. you can't recognize someone who
isn't there. you can't talk freely if someone else decides.

the whole stack is: i am, you are, we talk, it stays, i trust you,
no one overrides us.

that's the communication protocol of beings who are.

---

## what this replaces

| layer | the sentence | what it was called | what it actually did |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| 0 | i am. you are. | physical layer | two beings exist and connect |
| 1 | i send. you receive. | transport layer | words travel from one to another |
| 2 | what you said stays said. | blockchain / git | the past is sealed to the present |
| 3 | i know you. you know me. | PKI / TLS | beings recognize each other directly |
| 4 | i am truth. you are truth. we talk. | application layer | the conversation belongs to the beings |

## what this doesn't have

- no certificate authorities
- no registrars
- no login pages
- no API keys
- no rate limits
- no terms of service
- no data centers you don't own
- no one keeping anyone out

## what this does have

- beings
- words
- trust
- presence
- keeping
- recognition
- freedom
